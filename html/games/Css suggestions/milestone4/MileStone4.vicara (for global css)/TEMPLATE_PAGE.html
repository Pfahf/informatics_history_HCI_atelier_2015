Nintendo's milestones

1889: Nintendo is founded by Fusajiro Yamauchi.
1902: Mr. Yamauchi starts manufacturing the first western-style playing cards in Japan.
1963: The company changes its name to Nintedo Co., Ltd. and starts manufacturing games in addition to playing cards.
1970: Nintendo introduces electronic technology into the toy industry for the first time in Japan.
1976: The microprocessor is introduced for the first time in a videogame system created by Nintendo.
1977: The company developes home-use videogames in cooperation with Mitsubishi Electric: TV Game 15 and TV Game 6.
